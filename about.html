It all started as a normal high school band in Summerlin, Las Vegas. In 2004, Ryan Ross and Spencer Smith were childhood friends who thought they were talented enough to start a band. Ryan Ross played guitar and was the lead singer at that time, while Spencer Smith played the drums. They both recrutited Brent Wilson to play bass and Brent Wilson then recrutited Brendon Urie to play guitar. The band began rehearsing in Spencer Smith's grandmother's living room as a Blink-182 cover band. After Ryan Ross heard how well Brendon Urie sang, he made Urie the lead singer.


The band signed a recording contract without ever playing a live live show, but this was the case with many bands in Las Vegas at the time. Ryan Ross and a huge fight with his father when he told him that he was dropping out college to focus on music full time. Brendon Urie was kicked out of his house when he told their parents he was committing to his band.


After the band made about three songs, they randomly decided to send them to Pete Wentz, the bassist of Fall Out Boy. Wentz drove to Las Vegas just to hear them play live. After hearing them rehearse, Wentz encouraged them to sign up to Fueled By Ramen. After Panic! At The Disco signed up, Wentz began promoting the Panic! as much as possible. Wentz even began wearing a "Pete at the Disco" shirt to his concerts!


The band moved to Maryland to record their first albumn called "A Fever You Can't Sweat Out". They worked non-stop for about a month and a half. After releasing their first album, began touring, despite being exhausted from working on the album. Eventually, Panic! At The Disco climbed their way up the charts with their popular song "I Write Sins Not Tragedies".


In 2006, Panic! At The Disco kicked out Brent Wilson. Wilson was not pleased with the situation and told MTV News his perspective of what had happened. Wilson stated that Spencer kicked him out through a phone call while Brendon and Ryan listened on speakerphone. Spencer Smith decided to e-mail MTV News and explain the decision to remove Brent. Smith said that Wilson was not doing his part; Brent was not contributing to the band with new music nor was he playing his part as bass. Urie had been recording all of Wilson's parts in the album. Wilson was replaced with Jon Walker.


After they finished touring, Painc! began working on their second albumn in Nevada. The band was three quarters done when they thought of a new idea. The band wanted a new, simple taste to their music, so they trashed their current project to work on their new idea. Eventually, the album "Pretty. Odd." was finished and released. The album sold well, despite some controversy. They wrapped their mircophones with flowers, used soft colored lights, and wore vest when they preformed to symbolize their new and different style of music. The band also began an eco-friendly movement as soon as they began touring. They recycled more, used recycled products, and donated to environmental organizations.


By the time the band began to work on their next album, some problems began to arise. In 2009, Ryan Ross and Spencer Smith had discussed and came to the conclusion that Ross and Walker would be leaving the band. This was mostly due to the fact that Ross and Walker wanted more of a retro-inspired rock music style while Urie wanted to explore the pop culture music style. These creative differences split the band in two, so Smith and Urie were left alone in the band.


When Ross and Walker left, other people had to fill in their spots during Panic's tours. Dallon Weekes was among those few, and he joined the band in 2010. Dallon did not contriubte to Panic's new album, but he was in charge of creating the cover art of the album. Finally, Panic! released their album called "Vices & Virtues".


After touring for Vices & Virtues, Panic! released their fourth album called "Too Weird to Live, Too Rare to Die!". When Panic! began touring, Spencer made an ammouncemnt in a letter to his fans that he was fighting addiction. Smith stated he was abusing alcohol and perscription medications since the recordings of "Pretty. Odd.". Smith preformed at a couple of shows before leaving to take care of himself. It wasn't until 2015 when Spencer offically left the band.



Brendon Urie continued to make music under the name of Panic! At The Disco. Although all original band members left, he kept the band alive. Weekes left in 2017 to work on a slol project. In the meantime, Urie had made two new albums. "Death of a Bachelor" in 2015 and "Pray for the Wicked" in 2018 have been released and are preforming well.
